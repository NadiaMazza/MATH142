1 Housekeeping

1.1 Syllabus

  • The limit of a function of a real variable.
  • Continuity of a function of a real variable.
  • The definition of the derivative as a limit.
  • The graphical interpretation of the derivative.
  • Rules of differentiation.
  • Derivatives of standard functions.
  • Higher-order derivatives.
  • Stationary points and their classification.
  • Techniques of differentiation.
  • L’Hôpital’s rule.
  • Definite and indefinite integrals.
  • Integration as antiderivative.
  • Integration of standard functions.
  • Techniques of integration.
  • Approximation of definite integrals using Taylor and Maclaurin series.
  • Selection of applications of integration.

1.4 Learning outcomes

Subject specific

On successful completion of this module, the student should be able to:

  1. understand the meaning of a derivative, both algebraically and graphically;
  2. differentiate simple functions, products and quotients and use a variety of techniques;
  3. use L’Hôpital’s rule to find limits of functions;
  4. understand the meaning of definite and indefinite integrals;
  5. integrate expressions directly, by parts and by substitution;
  6. apply integration to calculate physical quantities;
  7. find Taylor and Maclaurin series, and use these to estimate definite integrals;

General knowledge, understanding and skills

On successful completion of this module, the student should be able to use a range of mathematical techniques from differential and integral calculus to solve engineering problems, present their work in a clear and coherent manner Demonstrate basic problem solving skills
